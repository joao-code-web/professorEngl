Research Group in Theoretical Inorganic Chemistry

When we completed our doctorate in 1997, we were promptly received by Prof. Wagner B. De Almeida into his research group for a postdoctoral internship. At that time, the Laboratory of Computational Chemistry and Molecular Modeling (LQC-MM) was already consolidated with great national and international prestige. In 1999, we joined the permanent staff of the Department of Chemistry in the concentration area of Inorganic Chemistry. It was a great challenge: to establish the research line in Theoretical Chemistry in the concentration area of Inorganic Chemistry. At the beginning, we had the invaluable help of Prof. Wagner B. De Almeida, who made his laboratory and facilities available to us. He supported us in our initiatives, creating space for effective and productive collaboration. In this way, we participated in LQC-MM as an associate researcher until 2002. Our efforts to establish and consolidate our research line paid off. With new undergraduate and graduate students and the need for physical space for new equipment, a new research group emerged: GPQIT. The GPQIT, listed in CNPq's Research Directory and formally recognized by UFMG, aims to develop and apply theoretical methods in the study of inorganic systems of interest to the environment, catalysis, and the biological field.
